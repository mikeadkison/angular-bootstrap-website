Quadcopters

For several years at the beginning of college, I built and flew quadcopters. What follows is chronology of all of the quadcopters that I've had.

Hoten X

My first quadcopter. About 400mm diagonally. It has metal arms and body and a plastic body cover. It came ready-to-fly from Singapore. I had to buy another charger since the one it came with didn't charge the battery. It came with the Devo 7 Radio which I used for my next quadcopter. I taped a flip camera to it to get shaky aerial footage

Knuckle Quad

My next quadcopter was on a wooden frame (400mm diagonal) held together by plastic pieces. The frame was made by Flite Test. I bought the electronics for this one separately from HobbyKing and assembled the quadcopter myself. It was more powerful than my previous one and very stable in the air.

Eventually, I bought a small camera and video transmitter along with some video goggles which had a built-in video receiver. I put the camera on the knuckle quad and was able to fly in first-person, similar to flying a plane from the cockpit. I also bought a gadget which moved the camera on two axes in response to my head movements (detected by the goggles), so I was able to look around while flying.

Hubsan

At the same time that I was flying my wooden quadcopter, I also had a tiny plastic quadcopter that I flew line-of-sight.

Honeybadger Delrin Quadcopter

Eventually I felt the need to build another quadcopter. This time, I bought a 400mm delrin (plastic) frame and bigger motors.

But that one broke way too easily, so I bought a pre-built carbon-fibre quadcopter with a 220mm diagonal.

While the frame was unbreakable, the electronics were very breakable. After breaking about 10 propellers and getting mud in all four motors, I decided to take a break from quadcopters. My next flying toy will be a foam airplane with a camera on it. I haven't decided which one yet.
